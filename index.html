In memory

Soma

S. "Soma" Somasegar spent 27 years at Microsoft, rising to lead the Developer Division and shaping the tools — Visual Studio, .NET, generations of Windows — that a huge share of this room built their careers on. After retiring in 2015, he joined Madrona as a Managing Director, where for over a decade he was one of the most trusted mentors in this exact ecosystem: patient with first-time founders, generous with his time, and known across Seattle for making everyone he met feel like the most important person in the room. He passed away suddenly on May 19, 2026, at 59. He's survived by his wife, Akila, and their two daughters.

Constellation Night sits inside the ecosystem Soma spent a decade building up — so we'd like to hold it in his name, with his family's requested causes, the YWCA (King County/Snohomish) and the UN Foundation, receiving a share of whatever the evening raises or a matching donation from Vieu.

This section should be shared with Madrona before anything goes out publicly — a dedication like this deserves their blessing, and likely Akila's, before Vieu's name is attached to it.
Format

Drop in, not sit down

No fixed program, no forced seating — this is built for people bouncing between Tech Week events all night. Come for ten minutes or the full three hours.

↻

Always-on dome

The whole Madrona network is up there from the start — thousands of points, not just tonight's guest list. Every few minutes the view slowly pans and zooms into a different corner of it, so there's always something new overhead, whenever you arrive.

🍸

Open bar, no pitch

Full open bar the entire window. Nobody's asked to sit through a talk — the tablets and the dome do the work while people just mingle.

📱

Tablet stations

4–6 stations around the room, staffed lightly, so there's never a long wait — each one takes a guest under two minutes start to finish.

Social loop

What travels past the room

Two things guests can post without any prompting from staff: a phone photo of the dome itself (it's genuinely photogenic — no branding needed for people to want that shot), and their personal connection card from step 3, formatted to drop straight into a LinkedIn post. Both carry #SeattleTechWeek and a quiet Vieu mark, not a hard sell.
